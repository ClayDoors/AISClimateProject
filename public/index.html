EcoTrack

Introduction

One of the main problems in today’s society is climate change. Out of all the problems in climate change, arguably the biggest factor that increases it is the carbon footprint released when transporting products through different types of vehicles. Some of the biggest postal services in the world, like FedEx, USPS, and UPS, constantly release tons of Carbon Dioxide emissions every day. While it may be impossible to find that number, it is possible to find how much carbon dioxide emissions your order releases when ordering from FedEx, USPS, or UPS.

EcoTrack, our newly designed and state of the art programmed website, allows us to see this. EcoTrack takes in multiple variables, including the type of postal service, the transportation type, the vehicle model, and the distance. Then, it calculates the carbon dioxide emissions released by that product. It does this through the built in code we made mixed special APIS relating to the variables needed in order to calculate the numbers. The numbers will shock you, which is why there's also a tab that shows you how to decrease carbon dioxide not just through ordering, but in all sorts of ways. EcoTrack helps show anyone who orders products online (using FedEx, USPS, or UPS) the unfortunate harm their products cause to the environment, which is why it’s so unique.While there are many websites that tell their audience the harm transporting products causes on the environment, EcoTrack takes their personal order details(inputted by the user) to demonstrate the harm on the environment.

If nothing changes, carbon dioxide emissions will only get worse as time goes on. According to “Ourworldindata”, transportation accounts for around 24% of US Greenhouse gas emissions. Out of that 24%, around 53% comes from shipping these products, with trucks and lorries accounting for almost 30% (Ritchie, 2020).

Most people do not understand how much carbon dioxide emissions their orders release, even if their products are being transported in their state. By creating a website in which people can understand how much carbon dioxide emissions their order releases while also providing solutions on how to reduce their carbon footprint, we can help reduce the overall carbon emissions produced on this earth.

Impact and Preventions

Our website has the potential to form a huge positive impact on society. It would help in solving a global issue on the overall topic of climate change. This would function by informing people of the devastating carbon footprint postal services release when transporting items. Whether it's huge companies like Amazon, Ebay or something as small as your town mailman all these transportation companies release a form of carbon emission. This is heavily relevant in today's day and age due to the increase in accessibility of ordering items online. Due to this, our website helps to emphasize the urgency of this topic, by showing the carbon emissions which are released into the environment.

Furthermore, our topic will never be irrelevant or lose its importance due to carbon dioxide emissions only getting worse as time goes on, and a huge part of these emissions come from transporting products through different types of vehicles such as cargo planes, vans, and 18 wheelers. However, with the knowledge of how much emissions an order/transportation method is releasing, society will be influenced to push towards more stable and less harmful forms of transportation. Thus leading to impacting the overall carbon emissions on a global scale by aiming to change how items are transported.

We estimate that website hosting through render will be $19 to start + $25 for our actual website hosting. Our carbon interface calculations will use the startup option which will run $30 per month. Our Google Maps calculation requests will cost .2 cents per request estimating that it will cost $20 per month with 10,000 calculations. Therefore, our final upkeep costs will be ~$94 per month. After we have enough traction on our website we will be able to run ads on the website to ensure a stable income to help pay for the upkeep. The ads won't be disruptive and will at most be on the side of the page. With the maximum amount of ads being seen on the page at one time being 2. Furthermore, we are going to incorporate a donation area where users can pay for having ads removed and help maintain the website.
